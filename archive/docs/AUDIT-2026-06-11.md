# Adversarial Data-Quality Audit — BİST Knowledge Graph

**Date:** 2026-06-11 · **Auditor method:** every figure below was measured against the live `tmkg.kuzu` database (read-only copy), the cache reports in `data/cache/`, and the actual code in `src/tmkg/`. The Koç blast-radius demo was re-run, a mid-cap counterfactual was run, and a synthetic cross-holding cycle was injected into a scratch copy to observe failure behavior. Nothing here is hypothetical unless labeled as such.

**Live DB state measured:** 729 Company nodes (594 EQUITY_TRADED, 135 NON_EQUITY_ISSUER) · 1,747 debt Securities (547 TRF, 330 TRD/sukuk, 268 TRS, 602 XS) · 112 CONTROLS edges (101 GLEIF @0.95, 1 KAP @0.85, 10 SPV @0.70) · 14 HOLDS_STAKE · 104 instruments with nominal (all TRY, ₺64.8bn total).

---

## F1 — The headline money number is ~1–2% of plausible reality, and the "lower bound" framing does not save it

**Severity: CRITICAL**

**Measured.** Re-running the Koç blast radius (window 2026-06-08 → 2027-12-08): 13 members, 135 in-window instruments, **17 priced (12.6%)**, confident outstanding **₺16.9bn**. Of the 135 instruments, **81 are XS eurobonds with `nominal = NULL` AND `currency = NULL`** — they appear in the output as `UNKNOWN` currency counts and contribute nothing to any money figure, not even the upper-bound bucket (the amortizing-upper-bound mechanism in `outstanding.py` only engages when a nominal exists; for XS paper it never does, so the bucket is structurally empty — verified: `outstanding_upper_by_currency: {}`).

**Quantified impact.** Turkish bank benchmark eurobonds run roughly $300–650m apiece. 81 in-window XS instruments at a conservative $300–450m average is **$24–36bn** of refinancing need. At any plausible 2026 USDTRY level, that is **50–100× the ₺16.9bn confident figure**. YKB alone shows 88 instruments and a priced wall of ₺3.2bn — for one of Turkey's largest external borrowers, that number is off by well over an order of magnitude.

**Failure mode.** A user asks "what is the Koç group's 18-month wall?" and receives a formatted answer of ₺16.9bn with a coverage caveat. Anchoring does the rest. "Lower bound" is technically true and practically false: a bound that captures ~1–2% of the quantity is not a bound, it is noise with a disclaimer. Worse, the bound is *biased*, not just incomplete — it systematically excludes exactly the FX wholesale funding that dominates Turkish group refinancing risk, so the TRY-only total inverts the actual risk ranking (a money-market-funded factoring arm looks bigger than YKB's eurobond stack).

**Fix.** Three options in increasing order of honesty: (a) refuse to emit a group money total when nominal coverage < some threshold (e.g. 50%), returning counts and currency mix only; (b) price XS instruments from a public source (bond OData feeds, prospectus scrape) — even issue-size-at-launch with a `fx-upper-bound` basis would beat NULL; (c) at minimum, populate `currency` for XS paper (it's recoverable from the MKK description) so the output says "81 instruments, mostly USD/EUR, unpriced" instead of "UNKNOWN". The current output shape makes the most dangerous misreading the easiest one.

## F2 — 27% of MKK debt instruments never entered the graph, silently, at the issuer-matching stage

**Severity: CRITICAL**

**Measured.** `mkk_debt_report.json`: 2,296 reference securities, **1,683 written**. The missing **613 (26.7%)** belong to 53 unmatched issuers. The graph does not contain them, does not count them in any denominator, and the blast-radius docstring's "2,296 instruments" framing hides this. Top losses: DİBS sovereign book (303 — arguably correct to exclude, but it's an accident of `no-brand-overlap`, not a policy), **Ziraat Bankası bank bills (119, `entity-type-conflict`)**, **Denizbank (50)**, TEB (18), Destek Yatırım Bankası (18), Katılım Varlık Kiralama (18).

**Failure mode.** This is worse than the documented "308 instruments without parsed maturity" — those at least exist as nodes. These 613 are invisible. Any analysis touching state-bank or Denizbank/TEB paper returns confidently *zero*. And the exclusion is a side effect of fuzzy-matcher thresholds, not a curated decision: sovereign debt fell out because "DİBS OPERASYONEL ÜYE" doesn't share brand tokens with anything, not because someone decided sovereigns are out of scope.

**Fix.** Split the unmatched bucket into policy exclusions (DİBS → explicit blocklist with a reason code) and match failures (Ziraat, Denizbank, TEB → manual alias table; 10–15 aliases recover ~230 instruments). Surface "instruments excluded at ingest: N" in every blast-radius result so the denominator is honest.

## F3 — 47% of in-window debt sits under issuers no group query can ever reach

**Severity: CRITICAL**

**Measured.** In the current 18-month window: 1,424 in-window instruments graph-wide; **671 (47%) belong to 118 issuers with no CONTROLS or SUBSIDIARY_OF edge in either direction**. Known nominal under unconnected issuers: ₺6.1bn vs ₺24.8bn connected — and the unconnected side is mostly *unpriced*, so the true skew is larger. The unconnected list is not noise: EXIMB (77 instruments), KT Sukuk VK (69 — Kuveyt Türk's funding vehicle), Emlak VK (61) + Emlak Katılım VK (26), **ZOREN/Zorlu Enerji (19 — a leveraged group with a well-known refinancing history whose apex, Zorlu Holding, is unlisted and therefore excluded by the in-universe rule)**, ZKB VK (18), Vakıf VK (12).

**Failure mode.** The in-universe-only design (110 external parents logged, not created) means any group whose apex is unlisted — Zorlu, Fiba, most participation-bank sukuk programs — **can never be assembled, by construction**. A user querying ZOREN gets a single-node "group." The blast-radius docstring discloses a version of this, but the numbers in it are stale (says "~134 of 196"; reality is 127 of 199 issuers, 47% of in-window paper) and the per-query output does not say "the following large issuers are structurally invisible to this query."

**Fix.** Create external parents as stub nodes (`is_listed=false`, no pricing) — the data is already in `gleif_parents.json` and the SPV report's 65 `no_in_graph_parent` rows. This is the single highest-leverage change in the backlog: it converts ~65 orphaned SPVs into assembled groups at zero new-data cost.

## F4 — "Multi-hop contagion" is not actually being exercised; the graph is a 1-hop lookup in practice

**Severity: HIGH**

**Measured.** Koç group hop distribution: **11 of 13 members at hop 1, one at hop 2** (YKR, reached only via an SPV-inference edge). 10 of the 112 CONTROLS edges are GLEIF ultimate-consolidation shortcuts that parallel an existing 2-hop path, and GLEIF direct edges already point apex→subsidiary for most members. `max_hops=6` is decorative; the same result would come from `max_hops=1` plus one edge.

**Failure mode.** Not a wrong-number problem — membership is correct — but a wrong-capability-claim problem. `control_hops` is presented as structural distance and reads as "contagion depth"; it actually measures "did GLEIF write a shortcut," which is an artifact of the source, not of Turkish group structure (KCHOL→YKB→Yapı Kredi Faktoring is a real 2-tier chain that the graph reports as two 1-hop members). Anyone using `control_hops` to weight contagion decay would be weighting noise.

**Fix.** Either materialize only direct edges and *derive* ultimate control at query time, or keep both but compute `control_hops` over the direct-edge subgraph (`extraction_method <> 'gleif-ultimate'` — requires tagging which GLEIF edges are ultimate vs direct; the loader knows but the edge doesn't say).

## F5 — The mid-cap counterfactual returns a confidently empty answer

**Severity: HIGH**

**Measured.** Seeding the blast radius at DNFIN (an unconnected debt issuer, 3 instruments): **members=1, instruments=3, outstanding={}, coverage=0.0**. No warning, no distinction between "this company is genuinely standalone" and "the graph has no edges here." The same call shape and the same report schema as the Koç showcase.

**Failure mode.** The system's usefulness is inversely correlated with how much you need it. For the large GLEIF-covered conglomerates (where any analyst already knows the group structure), it assembles nicely. For the mid-cap SPV-funded issuers where graph-assembled contagion would add real value, it returns near-empty results that are indistinguishable from "no risk." All demos use KOCFN/AKBNK/KCHOL; the demo set is precisely the subset where the system works.

**Fix.** Add a graph-coverage preamble to every result: seed's edge count, whether the seed appears in the SPV `no_in_graph_parent` or MKK `unmatched` lists, and an explicit `coverage_class: assembled | partial | blind`. Refuse the "group_total" key for `blind`.

## F6 — Cycles don't hang the engine; they silently corrupt the answer (verified by injection)

**Severity: HIGH (latent — no cycle in current data, but undetected by design)**

**Measured.** Current graph has zero CONTROLS cycles (checked lengths 2–3; the 202 CONTROLS/SUBSIDIARY_OF "cycles" are the intended reciprocal pairs). I then injected KOCFN→KCHOL into a scratch copy to simulate a cross-holding. Result: no infinite loop (Kuzu's variable-length match uses trail semantics), **but `resolve_group_root(KOCFN)` returned KOCFN itself as apex** — because the WHERE clause defines apex as "no incoming CONTROLS," and inside a cycle *no node qualifies*, so the function falls through to "seed is its own root." Silent, plausible-looking, wrong.

Also found while reading the function: the docstring says it walks up "via CONTROLS reversed *and* SUBSIDIARY_OF" — **the actual Cypher only traverses CONTROLS**. Doc/code divergence in the core resolver. Today this is masked because GLEIF writes both directions, but the 2 KAP-only SUBSIDIARY_OF edges are already invisible to root resolution.

**Failure mode.** Turkish cross-shareholdings are real (İş Bankası complex being the canonical case). The day one enters via the KAP-subsidiary loader, every member of that group quietly becomes its own apex and group totals fragment — with no error, no log line, no test failure.

**Fix.** Three lines of defense, all cheap: (1) a post-load integrity check that counts CONTROLS cycles and fails loudly; (2) redefine apex as "no incoming edge *from outside the candidate's own strongly connected component*" or collapse SCCs before rooting; (3) align the docstring or the query — pick one.

## F7 — Confidence is theater: written, gated, and then never read by anything

**Severity: HIGH**

**Measured.** `grep` over `src/tmkg/analytics/`: **no analytics code reads `confidence`** — not for filtering, weighting, path-compounding, or reporting. The output schema has no provenance breakdown. Mix in the live graph: 101 edges @0.95, 1 @0.85, 10 @0.70 (9% inference-tier). Worst current path: 0.95 × 0.70 ≈ 0.66 (KCHOL→YKB→YKR); no 0.70-chains exist *yet*, but nothing prevents them — the SPV loader would happily chain inferences as the universe grows.

**Concrete false-precision case found:** the SPV inferencer wrote KCHOL→KSFIN ("KOÇ STELLANTİS FİNANSMAN") as a control edge at 0.70. The name itself announces a joint venture; sole-control attribution to KCHOL is exactly the error class name-inference cannot see. Its ₺-wall is folded into the Koç group total at par with GLEIF-audited subsidiaries, indistinguishable in the output.

**Failure mode.** A user cannot answer "how much of this ₺16.9bn rests on naming-convention guesses?" from the output. (Answer, measured: KSFIN + YKR contribute ₺3.0bn of the ₺16.9bn confident total — **18% of the headline number rides on 0.70-confidence edges**, and nothing in the report says so.)

**Fix.** Minimum viable: per-member `min_path_confidence` and a group total split by provenance tier ("GLEIF-confirmed: ₺13.9bn; inference-attached: +₺3.0bn"). Path-product weighting can wait; tier-splitting cannot.

## F8 — Missingness is one cohort, not random — and that cohort carries 70% of the debt payload

**Severity: MEDIUM (correctly understood) / HIGH (if anyone treats per-company coverage as uniform)**

**Measured.** The three gaps are nearly the same companies: of 142 ISIN-less companies, 131 are NON_EQUITY_ISSUER; **all 123 sector-less companies are NON_EQUITY_ISSUER**; 122 companies miss both ISIN and sector. The NEI cohort (135 companies) is the SPV/delisted-issuer population — and it issues **1,229 of 1,747 (70%) of all debt instruments**. So the identity-richest nodes (listed equities) carry the least of the risk payload, and the identity-poorest carry most of it. Keeping NEIs in the graph and traversable is *correct* for the use case — they are the funding vehicles — but any sector-sliced or equity-priced analysis silently covers only the 30% minority of the debt book. The 11 EQUITY_TRADED companies with no ISIN and the 1 with no sector are residual noise.

**Failure mode.** "Refinancing wall by sector" style queries will look complete (606/729 companies classified!) while actually classifying issuers of only ~30% of instruments — the sector join drops the SPVs on the floor without saying so.

**Fix.** Propagate sector from control parent to NEI children (`sector_basis='inherited-from-parent'`), or at minimum report instrument-weighted (not company-weighted) coverage next to every sector-dimensional result.

## F9 — The "150 ambiguous ISINs" problem is actually an 11-company problem — but it includes EKGYO and İSDMR

**Severity: MEDIUM**

**Measured.** Of the 150 ambiguous tickers in `bist_isin.json`, only **11 are graph companies** (the rest are funds/non-universe lines, correctly out of scope). None of the 11 received an ISIN. The 11 include **EKGYO (Emlak Konut GYO — one of the largest REITs and the flagship state-linked property issuer) and ISDMR (İskenderun Demir Çelik — top-tier market cap)**, plus ODAS, KRONT, MERIT, et al. The ambiguity pattern (TRE…00019 vs …00027) is multiple share lines — resolvable by a human in minutes per name.

**Failure mode.** Docs overstate the problem (150) while understating its concentration: by market cap, the 11 dropped names are dominated by 2 structurally significant issuers. EKGYO is also a GYO with meaningful debt-market relevance — exactly the entity type the prompt suspected.

**Fix.** A 11-row manual disambiguation file closes this permanently. Estimated effort: under an hour. The fact that it hasn't been done suggests no one has run an equity-side analysis that needed it yet — which is itself evidence about how much of the system has seen real use.

## F10 — Maturity data: the documented numbers don't match the live graph, in *both* directions

**Severity: MEDIUM**

**Measured.** The documented "1,988/2,296 parsed maturity" does not describe the graph. Reality: 1,683 MKK instruments loaded, of which only **4 lack maturity** (conf 0.0, method 'none' — VakıfBank ×2, YKB, Teknosa); the other ~300 unparsed-maturity instruments are inside the 613 that never loaded (F2). There are **no conf=0.5 multi-date maturities in the live graph** — the documented "take-latest at 0.5" path (`ddmmyyyy-multi` in the adapter) exists in code but currently fires on zero loaded rows. All loaded maturities sit at 0.9 (regex) or 0.95.

The real issue: `refinancing_wall` filters `maturity_date IS NOT NULL` and **never reads `maturity_confidence`** — a 0.9 regex parse and a 0.5 take-latest guess would be indistinguishable in output. The gate is armed but the alarm isn't wired. On check digits: a failed ISO 6166 check digit means the *string* is corrupted (truncation, OCR, column-shift), not that the instrument is fake — and the check digit catches all single-char errors but only ~90% of random multi-char corruptions. If ~50 detectably-bad rows existed in a source file, expect on the order of ~5 *undetectably* bad ISINs that validated by luck. Note also: `mkk_debt_report.json` shows `quarantined_isins: 0` for the current run — the "~50 quarantined" claim isn't reproducible from the artifacts on disk; either it referred to an earlier file version or it's stale documentation. Flag whichever it is.

**Fix.** Pass `maturity_confidence` through to the wall output (min/mean per member); reconcile the 2,296/1,988/~50 documented figures against the actual run reports — the README-level numbers and the artifacts have drifted apart, which in an audit context is itself a finding.

## F11 — Staleness: IN_SECTOR has literally no properties; caches have no TTL; the universe is closed-world

**Severity: MEDIUM (rises with every passing month)**

**Measured.** `IN_SECTOR` carries **zero properties** — no `as_of`, no `source`, no confidence. The sector layer is a snapshot of one Excel exported 2026-06-07 with no staleness marker *anywhere in the graph itself* (only in a cache report JSON). GLEIF caches (`gleif_lookups.json`) are keyed by name + `matcher_version` with **no TTL and no LEI-record-date check** — a renamed or merged entity returns its pre-merger match forever, and `matcher_version` only invalidates when *your code* changes, not when *reality* changes. `discover_kap_issuances --from-graph` harvests only known issuers; combined with no scheduled `--seed` re-run, a 2026-H2 IPO or a new SPV is invisible until someone manually reseeds. KAP reshuffles sector memberships and new listings arrive monthly; at BİST's recent listing pace, expect the 729-company universe to be ~2–4% stale per quarter.

**Failure mode.** Each layer degrades silently and at a different rate, and nothing in any query result reveals data age. Six months from now the system will answer with identical confidence and meaningfully worse data.

**Fix.** `as_of` on IN_SECTOR (schema change, trivial); `fetched_iso` surfaced in every analytics result header; a TTL (e.g. 90 days) on GLEIF name-match cache entries; a scheduled reseed. None of this is hard; all of it is unglamorous, which is why it's missing.

## F12 — The test suite cannot catch the bugs that matter

**Severity: MEDIUM**

**Measured.** 113 test functions across 13 files. Live-network tests are skip-on-unreachable (`pytest.skip("KAP unreachable…")`) — in CI without network, they *pass by skipping*, and the suite is dominated by synthetic fixtures. The class of defects this suite structurally cannot catch is exactly the class found above: the MKK matcher silently dropping Ziraat's 119 instruments (a data-shape fact, not a logic bug), all 602 XS bonds landing unpriced *and* currency-less, documented metrics drifting from run artifacts, GLEIF cache staleness. Every one of those passes 113/113 today.

**Fix.** Add invariant tests against the *production* run reports, not fixtures: "unmatched debt_count ≤ X% of reference," "no EQUITY_TRADED company with >N debt instruments lacks a control edge," "documented coverage table regenerated from reports, never hand-written." Cheap, high-yield, and they would have caught F2 and F10 mechanically.

# Overall assessment

**This is a well-engineered research prototype that is not ready for investment research, and its biggest risk is that it looks ready.** The craftsmanship is real: the `outstanding.py` basis separation (bullet / upper-bound / unpriced / matured) is genuinely good design, provenance is recorded everywhere, run reports are honest, and the team clearly knows where several bodies are buried (the docstrings disclose more than most production systems do). That honesty is the system's best feature.

But for the stated use case — group refinancing contagion — the current state fails on the three things that matter most, all measured, none hypothetical:

1. **The money is missing.** The headline figure captures ~1–2% of the plausible Koç wall because the dominant risk channel (FX eurobonds) is unpriced and currency-unlabeled. A lower bound this far below the truth, presented in a formatted report, is a liability, not a caveat (F1).
2. **The graph is missing.** 27% of the debt universe never loaded (F2) and 47% of in-window paper is unreachable by any group query (F3). The system works precisely on the conglomerates where an analyst least needs it, and returns confident emptiness on the rest (F5).
3. **The uncertainty is missing.** Confidence is recorded and then ignored; 18% of the Koç headline rides on naming-convention inference — including a JV misattributed as sole control — with no marking in the output (F7). The apex resolver corrupts silently under cross-holdings (F6).

What it IS good for today: a *where*-map of TRY money-market refinancing pressure (counts and timing) across large GLEIF-covered groups, and an honest scaffold for the system it could become. What it is NOT: a source of any number with a ₺ sign in front of it that goes into a memo.

**Gate before any real use** (in order of leverage): external-parent stub nodes (F3), MKK alias table + ingest-exclusion reporting (F2), provenance-tier split + coverage-class refusal logic in blast-radius output (F1/F5/F7), cycle integrity check (F6). All four are days, not months. Until then, every blast-radius report should carry, in the output itself rather than a docstring: *"counts are meaningful; totals are not."*
